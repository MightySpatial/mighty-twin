Probe — interior navigation

A single primitive (NavigableSpace) for the inside of pipes, rooms, pits, and tunnels. The camera is a point particle that respects walls — hard collision and a soft proximity damp give the feel of natural physical constraint, like the user IS their eye.

Sibling paradigm: Street View. Same Fly-widget step binding, different geometry source (Google panoramas). Cross-flow tables in §11 of PROBE.md and illustrated in the flow mockups below.

Constraint feel — the "I am my eye" feedback

Two layers.
  • Hard collide: the camera origin is a point. Cannot cross a surface. Motion projects onto the surface tangent (collide-and-slide).
  • Soft damp: as distance drops below collision_threshold (default 0.3 m), velocity toward the wall is damped with a spring. Visually: vignette darkens on the wall side.
Net effect: lean against a wall, slide along it, can't punch through. Rapid input near a wall gives perceptible resistance — the hand feels the wall through the camera response.
